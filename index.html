About Me

Hi, my name is Alex Plamadeala. It all started over 10 years ago when I did my first Taekwon-do class, 5 years later I did my first Kickboxing class and today I have black belts in both sports. Somewhere along the path I set foot in the gym for the first time as a means to assist my fighting. This later manifested itself into a deep passion for strength and conditioning. I now compete as a Powerlifter while fighting nationally and internationally at the highest level in Taekwon-do and Kickboxing with aspirations to one day step out onto a Bodybuilding stage.

At the same time, I'm a full-time college student and I work part-time. I'm also like any other young college student, I like to go out, party and have a good time. I try balance all of this with fitness and in my opinion, I do a pretty damn good job of it.

A few years ago I stumbled across what's probably one of my biggest passions in life, YouTube. This has given me a means to document not only my progress in terms of fitness, but my life in general. It allows me to put myself out there, give people someone they can relate to and hopefully, in the grand scheme of things, make a positive impact on the world.

Lifting

I've been in the gym for the last 5-6 years however I started taking it a lot more seriously about 2-3 years ago. I am a nationally ranked Powerlifter in the Irish Powerlifting Federation. I mainly train for strength, but with strength comes size and aesthetics.

Fighting

I've been in martial arts since I was 8 years old starting in Taekwon-do then Kickboxing when I was 13. With over 10 years of fighting experience I compete nationally and internationally at the highest levels. I attribute a lot of my discipline to the invaluable lessons martial arts has taught me over the years.

Coaching

I am not nor do I claim to be an expert on training and nutrition (and neither are the majority of coaches). However, I know how to get people results, whether it's building muscle, losing body fat, getting stronger or doing a combination of the three. I employ an evidence-based approach to training and nutrition, a strong attention to detail to all programs and I do so in a way that is completely customised and directly applicable to each individual's life.

College

I would consider myself an academic because simply, I love education. I currently study Commerce in University College Dublin. I believe in constantly learning and educating yourself, and I especially apply this to training and nutrition so I am always providing the best service possible.
